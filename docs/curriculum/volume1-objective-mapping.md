# Volume 1 Objective Mapping

## Purpose

This document maps the learning objectives of *Audit Data Analysis – Volume 1* to the three competency levels defined in the Competency Model:

- Technical Skills (TS)
- Statistical Reasoning (SR)
- Professional Judgment (PJ)

The purpose of this mapping is to:

- verify that all competencies are represented throughout the book;
- identify competency gaps;
- support chapter redesign;
- guide the development of exercises, concept questions, and audit questions;
- support curriculum alignment.

This document does not replace the chapter learning objectives.

Instead, it provides a higher-level view of how those objectives contribute to the educational philosophy of Volume 1.

---

# Competency Definitions

## Technical Skills (TS)

The ability to perform statistical analyses correctly.

Guiding question:

> How do I perform the analysis?

Typical verbs:

- calculate;
- estimate;
- execute;
- construct;
- implement.

---

## Statistical Reasoning (SR)

The ability to explain, justify, interpret, and evaluate statistical methods.

Guiding question:

> Why is this method appropriate?

Typical verbs:

- explain;
- compare;
- justify;
- interpret;
- evaluate.

---

## Professional Judgment (PJ)

The ability to evaluate statistical evidence and determine which conclusions are supported.

Guiding question:

> What conclusions are supported by the available evidence?

Typical verbs:

- assess;
- evaluate;
- conclude;
- determine;
- justify.

# Volume-Level Competency Distribution

## Technical Skills

Volume 1 develops the ability to perform:

- probability calculations;
- estimation procedures;
- sampling applications;
- regression analyses;
- anomaly detection methods.

---

## Statistical Reasoning

Volume 1 develops the ability to:

- select methods;
- evaluate assumptions;
- interpret uncertainty;
- compare approaches;
- assess limitations.

---

## Professional Judgment

Volume 1 develops the ability to:

- evaluate evidence;
- assess uncertainty;
- determine supported conclusions;
- determine unsupported conclusions;
- avoid overinterpretation.

Professional Judgment in Volume 1 focuses on evidence evaluation rather than audit response.

# Validation Against the Competency Model

The learning objectives across Volume 1 demonstrate a clear progression:

```text
Technical Skills
        ↓
Statistical Reasoning
        ↓
Professional Judgment
```

Students first learn how to perform analyses.

They then learn how to interpret and justify those analyses.

Finally, they learn how to evaluate the evidence generated by those analyses and determine which conclusions are supported.

This progression is consistent with the Competency Model and the educational objectives of Volume 1.

---

# Capstone Outcome

A student completing Volume 1 should be able to:

### Technical Skills

Perform modern statistical analyses used in auditing.

### Statistical Reasoning

Explain, justify, and evaluate statistical methods and results.

### Professional Judgment

Evaluate statistical evidence and determine which conclusions are supported by that evidence.

Students are therefore prepared for Volume 2, where the focus shifts from evidence evaluation to evidence-based audit decision-making.
