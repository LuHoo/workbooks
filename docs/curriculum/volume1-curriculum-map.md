# Volume 1 Curriculum Map

## Purpose

This document maps the learning outcomes of *Audit Data Analysis – Volume 1* to the three competency levels defined in the Competency Model:

1. Technical Skills
2. Statistical Reasoning
3. Professional Judgment

The objective is to ensure that every chapter contributes to the development of all three competencies while maintaining an appropriate balance between statistical rigor and audit relevance.

---

# Volume 1 Educational Objective

Upon completion of Volume 1, students should be able to:

- perform statistical analyses used in auditing;
- understand and justify statistical methods;
- use statistical evidence to support audit conclusions.

The book therefore progresses from:

```text
Calculation
      ↓
Interpretation
      ↓
Audit Conclusion
```

rather than ending with the statistical output itself.

# Learning Objective Assessment Strategy

The three competency levels are assessed through different educational mechanisms.

The objective is not that every learning objective is assessed through both an exercise, a concept question and an audit question.

Instead, each competency is primarily developed and assessed through the format that best matches its nature.

| Competency | Primary Assessment Mechanism |
|------------|------------------------------|
| Technical Skills | Exercises |
| Statistical Reasoning | Exercises and Concept Questions |
| Professional Judgment | Audit Questions |

---

## Technical Skills

Technical Skills are best developed through active execution.

Students learn by:

- performing calculations;
- using software;
- reproducing results;
- implementing methods.

Therefore, Technical Skills are primarily addressed through exercises in the R and Python workshops.

Typical activities include:

- calculating confidence intervals;
- determining sample sizes;
- fitting regression models;
- conducting goodness-of-fit tests.

---

## Statistical Reasoning

Statistical Reasoning bridges execution and interpretation.

Students learn to:

- explain assumptions;
- compare methods;
- justify methodological choices;
- interpret statistical findings.

Therefore, Statistical Reasoning should be addressed through both:

- workshop exercises; and
- concept questions.

Typical questions include:

- Why is this estimator preferred?
- Which assumptions are required?
- Why is this approximation valid?
- How should these results be interpreted?

---

## Professional Judgment

Professional Judgment concerns the use of statistical evidence in an audit context.

Students learn to:

- evaluate evidence;
- assess uncertainty;
- determine whether conclusions are justified;
- recommend further procedures.

These objectives are best addressed through audit questions and case-based reflection rather than computational exercises.

Typical questions include:

- Does the evidence support the audit conclusion?
- Is additional audit work required?
- How should these findings be communicated?
- What residual uncertainty remains?

Professional Judgment questions should typically be case-based and discussion-oriented.

# Educational Component Mapping

Different educational components serve different purposes within Volume 1.

## Workshops

The primary role of workshops is the development of Technical Skills.

Secondary emphasis is placed on Statistical Reasoning.

Structure:

### Part A – Execute

Primary competency:

Technical Skills

### Part B – Interpret

Primary competency:

Statistical Reasoning

---

## Concept Questions

The primary role of concept questions is the development of Statistical Reasoning.

Typical focus:

- assumptions;
- interpretation;
- method selection;
- limitations.

---

## Audit Questions

The primary role of audit questions is the development of Professional Judgment.

Typical focus:

- evaluation of evidence;
- audit conclusions;
- additional procedures;
- communication of findings.

---

## Cases

Cases provide the integration point of all three competencies.

Students should:

1. Perform an analysis.
2. Interpret the results.
3. Draw an audit conclusion.

Cases therefore connect Technical Skills, Statistical Reasoning and Professional Judgment.

# Question Taxonomy

Volume 1 distinguishes three categories of student activities.

## Exercises (TS)

Purpose:

Perform the analysis.

Typical verbs:

- calculate;
- construct;
- estimate;
- execute;
- reproduce.

Primary competency:

Technical Skills.

---

## Concept Questions (SR)

Purpose:

Understand and justify the analysis.

Typical verbs:

- explain;
- compare;
- interpret;
- justify;
- assess.

Primary competency:

Statistical Reasoning.

---

## Audit Questions (PJ)

Purpose:

Use the analysis to support an audit decision.

Typical verbs:

- evaluate;
- conclude;
- recommend;
- defend;
- determine.

Primary competency:

Professional Judgment.

---

This distinction reflects the philosophy that not every learning objective should be assessed through the same educational mechanism.

Different competencies require different forms of learning and assessment.

# Capstone Outcome

A student completing Volume 1 should be able to:

### Technical Skills

Perform modern statistical analyses used in auditing.

### Statistical Reasoning

Explain, justify, and evaluate statistical methods and results.

### Professional Judgment

Translate statistical evidence into defensible audit conclusions.

# Volume 1 Positioning Statement

> Volume 1 develops the statistical foundation required for modern auditing.
>
> Students learn not only how to perform analyses, but also how to interpret their outcomes and how to use statistical evidence in support of professional audit judgments.
>
> The ultimate goal is statistically informed audit decision-making.
