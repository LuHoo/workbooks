# Competency Model

## Purpose

This document describes the educational philosophy underlying the *Audit Data Analysis* series.

The competency model serves as the foundation for:

- Volume 1;
- Volume 2;
- learning objectives;
- workshops;
- exercises;
- concept questions;
- audit questions;
- curriculum mapping;
- instructor materials;
- university positioning.

The objective is to develop professionals who can perform statistical analyses, reason about their appropriateness, and exercise sound professional judgment based on statistical evidence.

---

# Why a Competency Model?

Historically, statistics education focused heavily on computation.

Students learned how to:

- calculate probabilities;
- construct confidence intervals;
- determine sample sizes;
- perform hypothesis tests;
- build regression models.

These skills remain important.

However, modern statistical software and artificial intelligence increasingly automate the computational aspects of statistical analysis.

As a result, professional value is created not merely through calculation, but through:

- selecting appropriate methods;
- understanding assumptions;
- interpreting results correctly;
- evaluating uncertainty;
- determining what conclusions are supported by the evidence.

Consequently, statistics education for auditors must develop capabilities beyond technical execution.

The educational focus shifts from:

> How do I calculate this result?

towards:

> What does this result mean, and what conclusions does it support?

---

# The Three Competency Levels

The Audit Data Analysis series is built around three interconnected competency levels.

```text
Professional Judgment
          ▲
          │
Statistical Reasoning
          ▲
          │
Technical Skills
```

The competencies are cumulative.

Professional Judgment depends upon Statistical Reasoning.

Statistical Reasoning depends upon Technical Skills.

Students cannot evaluate evidence appropriately without understanding statistical reasoning, and statistical reasoning requires a sound technical foundation.

---

# Level 1: Technical Skills

## Definition

Technical Skills are the abilities required to perform statistical analyses correctly.

Students learn:

- how statistical methods operate;
- how software implements statistical methods;
- how analyses are executed and reproduced.

The focus is on execution.

---

## Guiding Question

> How do I perform the analysis?

---

## Typical Activities

Students learn to:

- calculate statistical measures;
- estimate parameters;
- determine sample sizes;
- fit statistical models;
- perform hypothesis tests;
- execute analyses in R and Python;
- reproduce statistical output;
- validate computational results.

---

## Typical Verbs

- calculate
- construct
- estimate
- compute
- execute
- reproduce
- implement
- perform

---

## Example

A student can calculate a confidence interval or fit a regression model correctly.

Success is determined primarily by correct execution.

---

# Level 2: Statistical Reasoning

## Definition

Statistical Reasoning is the ability to understand, interpret, compare, and justify statistical methods and results.

The focus is on understanding.

Students learn that every statistical procedure has:

- assumptions;
- strengths;
- weaknesses;
- limitations;
- appropriate application domains.

---

## Guiding Question

> Why is this method appropriate?

---

## Typical Activities

Students learn to:

- select suitable methods;
- evaluate assumptions;
- compare alternatives;
- interpret uncertainty;
- assess the quality of evidence;
- explain statistical output;
- distinguish statistical significance from practical relevance.

---

## Typical Verbs

- explain
- compare
- interpret
- justify
- analyze
- assess
- distinguish
- evaluate

---

## Example

A student can explain:

- why a hypergeometric distribution is preferred over a binomial distribution;
- why regression is appropriate in a given situation;
- why a model should or should not be trusted.

Success is determined by quality of reasoning rather than the ability to perform calculations alone.

---

# Level 3: Professional Judgment

## Definition

Professional Judgment is the ability to evaluate statistical evidence and determine what conclusions are supported by that evidence.

Statistical procedures are no longer the objective.

They become tools that support professional evaluation.

The focus shifts from:

> What does the analysis show?

towards:

> What conclusions are justified by the available evidence?

---

## Guiding Question

> What does the available evidence allow the auditor to conclude?

---

## Typical Activities

Students learn to:

- evaluate evidence;
- assess uncertainty;
- determine the strength of conclusions;
- distinguish supported conclusions from unsupported conclusions;
- communicate limitations appropriately;
- avoid overinterpreting statistical results.

---

## Typical Verbs

- evaluate
- conclude
- determine
- assess
- defend
- communicate
- justify

---

## Example

A student can determine:

- whether available evidence supports a conclusion;
- how much uncertainty remains;
- whether a conclusion is stronger than the evidence justifies;
- whether important limitations remain.

Success is determined by the quality of evidence evaluation.

---

# Professional Judgment Across the ADA Series

Professional Judgment develops progressively across the Audit Data Analysis series.

Although Professional Judgment appears in both volumes, its role differs significantly between Volume 1 and Volume 2.

---

## Volume 1: Evidence Evaluation

In Volume 1, Professional Judgment focuses on evaluating statistical evidence.

Students learn to:

- assess the strength of evidence;
- evaluate residual uncertainty;
- determine which conclusions are supported;
- determine which conclusions are not supported;
- communicate limitations appropriately.

The emphasis is on evidence evaluation rather than audit action.

Central question:

> What does the available evidence allow the auditor to conclude?

Examples include:

- Does the evidence support the conclusion?
- How persuasive is the evidence?
- What uncertainty remains?
- What conclusions are not yet justified?

---

## Volume 2: Audit Decision-Making

In Volume 2, Professional Judgment focuses on deciding how the auditor should respond to available evidence.

Students learn to:

- determine appropriate responses;
- evaluate audit implications;
- design follow-up procedures;
- integrate evidence into audit strategy;
- defend audit decisions.

The emphasis shifts from evaluation to action.

Central question:

> Given the available evidence, what should the auditor do?

Examples include:

- Should additional procedures be performed?
- How should audit risk be reassessed?
- What is the appropriate audit response?
- How should the conclusion be incorporated into the audit strategy?

---

# Relationship Between the Volumes

The two volumes form a continuous progression.

## Volume 1

```text
Technical Skills
        ↓
Statistical Reasoning
        ↓
Evidence Evaluation
```

Students learn to understand and evaluate statistical evidence.

---

## Volume 2

```text
Evidence Evaluation
        ↓
Audit Decision-Making
        ↓
Professional Action
```

Students learn how audit professionals respond to that evidence.

# Relationship with Bloom's Taxonomy

The competency model complements Bloom's taxonomy.

The two frameworks should be used together.

| Competency Level | Dominant Bloom Levels |
|------------------|-----------------------|
| Technical Skills | Remember, Apply |
| Statistical Reasoning | Understand, Analyze |
| Professional Judgment | Evaluate, Create |

Bloom describes cognitive complexity.

The competency model describes professional capability.

Together they provide a complete educational framework.

---

# Implications for Educational Design

The three competencies should not be taught through identical educational mechanisms.

Different competencies require different forms of learning.

| Competency | Primary Learning Mechanism |
|------------|----------------------------|
| Technical Skills | Exercises |
| Statistical Reasoning | Exercises and Concept Questions |
| Professional Judgment | Audit Questions and Case Discussions |

Not every learning objective should appear in every educational format.

The learning mechanism should match the nature of the competency being developed.

# Educational Components

## Exercises

Primary Competency:

**Technical Skills**

Purpose:

> Can the student perform the analysis?

Examples:

- calculations;
- software implementation;
- model construction;
- statistical procedures.

---

## Concept Questions

Primary Competency:

**Statistical Reasoning**

Purpose:

> Can the student explain and justify the analysis?

Examples:

- assumptions;
- interpretation;
- method comparison;
- model evaluation.

---

## Audit Questions

Primary Competency:

**Professional Judgment**

Purpose:

> Can the student evaluate the evidence and determine what conclusions are supported?

Examples:

- assessment of evidence;
- evaluation of uncertainty;
- supported conclusions;
- limitations of conclusions.

---

## Cases

Cases integrate all three competency levels.

Students should:

1. Perform an analysis.
2. Interpret the analysis.
3. Evaluate the evidence.
4. Formulate a supported conclusion.

Cases therefore provide the integration point for the entire competency model.

# Implications for Volume 1

Volume 1 develops the statistical foundation required for audit data analysis.

Relative emphasis:

### Technical Skills

High

Students learn to perform statistical analyses.

### Statistical Reasoning

High

Students learn to interpret and justify statistical methods.

### Professional Judgment

Moderate

Students learn to evaluate evidence and determine supported conclusions.

---

## Volume 1 Graduate

A student completing Volume 1 should be able to:

1. Perform statistical analyses.
2. Explain and justify statistical methods.
3. Evaluate statistical evidence.
4. Determine what conclusions are supported by that evidence.

---

# Implications for Volume 2

Volume 2 focuses on applying statistical evidence within the audit process.

Relative emphasis:

### Technical Skills

Moderate

Assumed foundation from Volume 1.

### Statistical Reasoning

High

Students learn to assess evidence in increasingly complex situations.

### Professional Judgment

Very High

Students learn to integrate evidence into audit decision-making.

---

## Volume 2 Graduate

A student completing Volume 2 should be able to:

1. Evaluate statistical evidence.
2. Determine appropriate audit responses.
3. Defend audit decisions.
4. Integrate statistical evidence into audit methodology.

---

# Core Educational Principle

The Audit Data Analysis series is not intended to teach statistics for its own sake.

Its purpose is to develop professionals who can:

- perform analyses correctly;
- understand analyses appropriately;
- evaluate evidence responsibly;
- make sound professional decisions.

In an environment where software increasingly performs calculations automatically, these abilities become increasingly important.

---

# Positioning Statement

> Audit Data Analysis develops three essential competencies:
>
> **Technical Skills**, enabling students to perform statistical analyses;
>
> **Statistical Reasoning**, enabling students to understand and justify statistical methods; and
>
> **Professional Judgment**, enabling students to evaluate statistical evidence and apply it appropriately within the audit process.
>
> Together, these competencies support statistically informed audit decision-making.
