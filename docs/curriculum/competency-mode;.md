# Competency Model

## Purpose

This document describes the educational philosophy underlying the *Audit Data Analysis* series.

The competency model serves as the foundation for:

- Volume 1;
- Volume 2;
- learning objectives;
- workshops;
- review questions;
- instructor materials;
- curriculum mapping;
- positioning towards universities and professional education providers.

The model is intended to ensure that Audit Data Analysis develops not only technical competence, but also the ability to reason statistically and exercise professional judgment.

---

# Why a New Competency Model?

Traditionally, statistics education emphasized computational skills.

Students learned how to:

- calculate probabilities;
- construct confidence intervals;
- determine sample sizes;
- perform hypothesis tests;
- fit regression models.

While these skills remain important, the professional environment has changed fundamentally.

Modern software can perform statistical calculations almost instantaneously. Artificial intelligence and data analytics tools further reduce the effort required to execute statistical procedures.

As a result, the competitive advantage of auditors no longer lies primarily in performing calculations.

Instead, value is created through:

- selecting appropriate methods;
- understanding assumptions;
- interpreting results correctly;
- evaluating uncertainty;
- translating statistical evidence into audit conclusions.

The role of education therefore shifts from computational mastery alone to statistically informed professional decision-making.

---

# The Three Competency Levels

The Audit Data Analysis series is built around three interconnected competency levels.

```text
Professional Judgment
          ▲
          │
Statistical Reasoning
          ▲
          │
Technical Skills
```

The competencies are cumulative.

Professional Judgment depends on Statistical Reasoning.

Statistical Reasoning depends on Technical Skills.

A student cannot effectively exercise professional judgment without understanding statistical reasoning, and statistical reasoning requires sufficient technical foundation.

---

# Level 1: Technical Skills

## Definition

Technical Skills are the abilities required to perform statistical analyses correctly.

At this level, students learn how statistical methods work and how software can be used to execute them.

The focus is on execution.

---

## Guiding Question

> How do I perform the analysis?

---

## Typical Activities

Students learn to:

- calculate statistical measures;
- estimate parameters;
- determine sample sizes;
- fit statistical models;
- perform hypothesis tests;
- execute analyses in R and Python;
- reproduce statistical output;
- validate computational results.

---

## Typical Verbs

- calculate
- construct
- estimate
- compute
- execute
- reproduce
- implement
- perform

---

## Example

A student can calculate a 95% confidence interval.

At this level, success is determined by obtaining the correct result.

---

# Level 2: Statistical Reasoning

## Definition

Statistical Reasoning is the ability to understand, interpret, compare, and justify statistical methods and outcomes.

The focus shifts from execution to understanding.

Students learn that statistical procedures are not interchangeable.

Every method has:

- assumptions;
- strengths;
- weaknesses;
- limitations;
- conditions for use.

---

## Guiding Question

> Why is this method appropriate?

---

## Typical Activities

Students learn to:

- select suitable methods;
- assess assumptions;
- compare alternative approaches;
- interpret uncertainty;
- distinguish statistical from practical significance;
- evaluate model quality;
- explain the meaning of statistical output.

---

## Typical Verbs

- explain
- compare
- interpret
- justify
- analyze
- assess
- distinguish
- evaluate

---

## Example

A student can explain:

- why a hypergeometric distribution is preferred over a binomial distribution;
- why a regression model is appropriate for a substantive analytical procedure;
- why a confidence interval becomes narrower when sample size increases.

Success is determined by conceptual understanding rather than computational correctness alone.

---

# Level 3: Professional Judgment

## Definition

Professional Judgment is the ability to use statistical evidence in support of audit decisions.

At this level, statistical methods become tools rather than objectives.

The focus shifts from:

> What does the analysis show?

to

> What does the analysis mean for the audit?

---

## Guiding Question

> What conclusion should the auditor draw?

---

## Typical Activities

Students learn to:

- evaluate audit evidence;
- assess uncertainty;
- determine whether conclusions are justified;
- identify the need for additional procedures;
- communicate findings;
- defend decisions;
- balance statistical evidence with professional skepticism.

---

## Typical Verbs

- evaluate
- conclude
- recommend
- determine
- defend
- communicate
- justify
- assess

---

## Example

A student can determine whether:

- available evidence sufficiently supports an audit conclusion;
- additional testing is necessary;
- an observed anomaly warrants investigation;
- a model provides persuasive audit evidence.

Success is determined by the quality of professional decision-making.

---

# Relationship with Bloom's Taxonomy

The competency model does not replace Bloom's taxonomy.

Instead, the two frameworks complement each other.

| Competency Level | Dominant Bloom Levels |
|------------------|-----------------------|
| Technical Skills | Remember, Apply |
| Statistical Reasoning | Understand, Analyze |
| Professional Judgment | Evaluate, Create |

Both frameworks should be reflected in all learning objectives.

# Application to Volume 1

## Educational Goal

Volume 1 develops the statistical foundation required for audit data analysis.

Students learn:

- how statistical methods work;
- when they should be used;
- how audit conclusions can be supported with statistical evidence.

---

## Relative Emphasis

### Technical Skills

High emphasis.

Students develop practical competence in:

- estimation;
- sampling;
- regression;
- goodness-of-fit methods.

### Statistical Reasoning

High emphasis.

Students learn:

- method selection;
- assumptions;
- interpretation of uncertainty.

### Professional Judgment

Moderate emphasis.

Students begin connecting statistical outcomes to audit conclusions.

---

## Volume 1 Graduate

After completing Volume 1, a student should be able to:

1. Perform statistical analyses.
2. Explain statistical results.
3. Use statistical evidence to support audit conclusions.

---

# Application to Volume 2

## Educational Goal

Volume 2 focuses on the use of statistical evidence within the broader audit process.

The emphasis shifts from methodology to decision-making.

---

## Relative Emphasis

### Technical Skills

Moderate emphasis.

Assumed foundation from Volume 1.

### Statistical Reasoning

High emphasis.

Students evaluate techniques in increasingly complex settings.

### Professional Judgment

Very high emphasis.

Students apply statistical evidence within:

- risk assessment;
- audit planning;
- audit execution;
- audit evaluation;
- audit reporting.

---

## Volume 2 Graduate

After completing Volume 2, a student should be able to:

1. Integrate statistical evidence into audit methodology.
2. Defend audit decisions.
3. Apply professional judgment in uncertain environments.

# Implications for Book Design

Every chapter should contain elements addressing all three competency levels.

---

## Technical Skills Components

Examples:

- worked examples;
- formulas;
- software implementation;
- computational exercises.

---

## Statistical Reasoning Components

Examples:

- interpretation questions;
- comparison of methods;
- discussion of assumptions;
- analysis of limitations.

---

## Professional Judgment Components

Examples:

- audit evaluation sections;
- conclusion questions;
- client communication scenarios;
- follow-up procedure discussions.

---

# Implications for Workshops

Every workshop should follow a common structure.

## Part A: Execute

Perform the analysis.

Primary competency:

**Technical Skills**

---

## Part B: Interpret

Explain the output.

Primary competency:

**Statistical Reasoning**

---

## Part C: Evaluate

Determine audit implications.

Primary competency:

**Professional Judgment**

---

# Implications for Review Questions

Each chapter should contain three categories of review questions.

## Technical Skills

Focus:

> Can the student perform the analysis?

---

## Statistical Reasoning

Focus:

> Can the student explain and justify the analysis?

---

## Professional Judgment

Focus:

> Can the student use the analysis in an audit context?

---

# Implications for Cases

Cases should not end with statistical output.

Every case should conclude with four sections:

1. Statistical Result
2. Interpretation
3. Audit Evaluation
4. Professional Conclusion

This ensures that statistical analysis remains connected to audit decision-making.

---

# Core Educational Principle

The Audit Data Analysis series is not intended to teach statistics for its own sake.

Its purpose is to develop professionals who can:

- perform analyses correctly;
- interpret analyses responsibly;
- use analyses to support sound audit judgments.

In an environment where software increasingly performs calculations automatically, these capabilities become more—not less—important.

---

# Positioning Statement

> Audit Data Analysis develops three essential competencies:
>
> **Technical Skills**, enabling students to perform statistical analyses;
>
> **Statistical Reasoning**, enabling students to understand and justify statistical methods; and
>
> **Professional Judgment**, enabling students to translate statistical evidence into defensible audit conclusions.
>
> Together, these competencies support statistically informed audit decision-making.
